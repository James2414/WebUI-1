Why tech billionaires are crucial for
innovation (& aren’t actually a drain
on society)

Most major product breakthroughs are made by technology companies
started up by people who are now billionaires.

Next level

That billionaire status uniquely enables capacity for additional long-term,
next-level, build-up of major projects. Well beyond the original struggling forbr
air ‘startup’. But the best companies maintain the startup mentality.

Governments rarely invent things.


They may fund early stage research and they did in the case of the atom bomb. But they rarely get us to useable products.


We need billionaires to stick with risky projects for up to decades if it’s genuine practical innovation we’re after that keeps building on itself.


Like Tesla’s Masterplan to go from rich boys EV (Roadster) to almost affordable (Model S/X) to the EV for many (Model 3/Y) to the EV for all (the coming Model 2).


That was a 20 year plan. Only a single-minded, pig-headed billionaire can get that done.


Tech CEO number one shareholders have the best track record.


Commitees of smaller share founders usually just don’t cut it. They don’t have the single-mindedness required for long-term projects by definition.


You need pig-headed, one-tracked founders to do their thing sometimes.


But it’s not just financial incentive that drives them.

Billionaires aren’t actually a waste of resources

Billionaires – even the non-altruistic ones – seriously do not selfishly use more than 0.1% of their paper wealth.


Nobody can or could! There’s only so many jets you can use.


99.9% of their funds ultimately do just create jobs and new technologies that do not directly benefit these business owners.


So, the waste and inequity problem are nowhere near as serious as people think.


They’re after power to create Most tech billionsures live for the adrenaline of the creation of tech that never existed before.
